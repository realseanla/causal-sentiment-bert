This article presents the top-level of an ontology categorizing and generalizing best practices and quality criteria or measures for Linked Data.
It permits to compare these techniques and have a synthetic organized view of what can or should be done for knowledge sharing purposes.
This ontology is part of a general knowledge base that can be accessed and complemented by any Web user.
Thus, it can be seen as a cooperatively built library for the above cited elements.
Since they permit to evaluate information objects and create better ones, these elements also permit knowledge-based tools and techniques - as well as knowledge providers - to be evaluated and categorized based on their input/output information objects.
One top-level distinction permitting to organize this ontology is the one between content, medium and containers of descriptions.
Various structural, ontological, syntactical and lexical distinctions are then used.
